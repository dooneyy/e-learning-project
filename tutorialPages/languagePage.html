English

Parts of Speech

A part of speech is a term used in traditional grammar for the main categories into which words are classified according to their functions in sentences, such as nouns or verbs. Also known as word classes, these are the building blocks of grammar

Noun

A noun is a part of speech (or word class) that names or identifies a person, place, thing, quality, idea, or activity. Most nouns have both a singular and plural form, can be preceded by an article and/or one or more adjectives, and can serve as the head of a noun phrase.", body: "A noun or noun phrase can function as a subject, direct object, indirect object, complement, appositive, or object of a preposition. In addition, nouns sometimes modify other nouns to form compound nouns.

Types
  • Proper Noun
  • Collective Noun
  • Abstract Noun

Pronoun

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun, noun phrase, or noun clause. A pronoun can function as a subject, object, or complement in a sentence

Types
  • Demostrative Pronoun
  • Interrogative Pronoun
  • Indefinite Pronoun

Verd

A verb is the part of speech that describes an action or occurrence or indicates a state of being. Verbs can display differences in tense, mood, aspect, number, person, and voice

Types
  • Action Verb
  • Linking Verb
  • Helping
