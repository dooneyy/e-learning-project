Arithmetic Progression

An arithmetic progression (AP), also called an arithmetic sequence, is a sequence of numbers which differ from each other by a common difference. For example, the sequence 1, 3, 5, 7, 9…. is an arithmetic sequence with the common difference of 2. The figure below represents an arithmetic progression in which the terms are increasing In each pattern, there is a first term, a and a common difference, d between consecutive terms. Notice in figure 1b that d will be positive since the terms increase as the sequence progresses. The following sequence represents a general arithmetic progression; a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ……….

nth term of an AP. If Un denotes the nth term of a sequence, then; Un = a + (n – 1)d This can also be shown by observing the pattern of the terms in the general arithmetic progression, thus

1st term = a
2nd term = a + d
3rd term = a + 2d
4th term = a + 3d
5th term = a + 4d
6th term = a + 5d
nth tem = a + (n – 1)d

Geometric Progression

A geometric progression or GP is a sequence where each new term after the first is obtained by multiplying the preceding term by a constant, r, called common ratio. The sequence 5, 10, 20, 40, 80, has a first term of 5 and a common ratio between terms of 2, i.e. the ratio of the second term to the first is 10:5 (or 2:1), that of the third term to the second term is 20:10 (or 2:1), and so on The following sequence represents a general geometric progression: a, ar, ar2, ar3, ar4 nth term of an GP: The nth term of a GP is given by Un where, Un = arn – 1 This can be shown by observing the pattern of the terms in the genera GP

1st term = a
2nd term = ar
3rd term = ar2
4th term = ar3
5th term = ar4
nth term = arn-1
